Welcome to the

The Department offers a wide variety of mathematics and statistics courses, ranging from the developmental and remedial level to the first two years of college or university. The courses support the development of basic skills mathematics, as well as support a variety of certificate programs and associate’s degrees. The courses also prepare students to transfer to a college or university to major in mathematics, engineering, the physical or life sciences, business, liberal studies, and a host of other majors.

Tips For Success

  • Start homework early and put in appropriate effort - There is a famous quote which says, "Never put off till tomorrow what you can do the day after tomorrow". Although procasination is common, it is important to do the homework excercises as you are studying the material.
  • Use help from tutors appropriately - Some students may do all their homework sitting with a tutor, but using a tutor's help only when neccessary is a much better learning technique. During exam time, tutors won't be able to help, so using a tutor only when neccessary is a helpful study technique.
  • Use office hours wisely - Nobody's perfect and making mistakes is normal. It's okay to get stuck on a problem, as long as you talk to your professor about it during office hours.
  • Read the textbook - Some students don't read the textbook except when looking for answers, this is not a good idea. Reading the problems and solutions may be difficult, but this is an important process in developing knowledge.
  • Aknowledge that math isn't memorization - Being successful in math inloves learning, not memorization. Being successful in mathematics means applying your knowledge to a variety of problems. Everything in mathematics has a story behind it, it is important to learn this story and not just use memory.
